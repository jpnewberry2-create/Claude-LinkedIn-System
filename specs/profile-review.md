## Findings — what the research says about high-performing profiles

- **The headline is the highest-impact field.** Appears in every search result, notification, and comment. 220 characters available; generic job titles waste it.
- **The first ~300 characters of the About section** appear in search previews and above "see more." Front-load with what someone would search for, not jargon.
- **LinkedIn now uses AI semantic search**, not just keyword matching. Depth and specificity matter, not stuffing.
- **The Featured section** is the portfolio at the top of the profile. Empty = wasted prime real estate.
- **The banner** is positioning territory at first glance. Default LinkedIn-blue signals "I haven't bothered."
- **Profile photo materially lifts views.** A clear, well-lit headshot is non-negotiable.
- **Skills section is a secondary keyword index** — the top 3 pinned skills carry the most weight.
- **Recent activity drives profile visibility.** Even one post per week boosts how often the profile surfaces. Posting *is* profile optimisation.

### The conflict to navigate

Almost every profile-optimisation guide online is written for **lead generation or job hunting**: booking links, "I help X do Y" headlines, problem-agitation-solution About sections. That collides with the locked rule: **profile is credibility, content is personality.**

- **Adopt:** front-loaded keywords, specific role description, clean photo, optimised skills, pinned best content in Featured, banner that reinforces positioning, recent activity.
- **Drop:** "I help X do Y" positioning, Calendly links, lead magnets, "Book a call" CTAs, problem-agitation-solution copy, anything that reads like a landing page.

---

## Benefits — why optimising the profile is worth doing

1. **It compounds with every post.** Every comment and post surfaces the headline.
2. **It converts content attention into follows.** People decide in 3–6 seconds.
3. **It surfaces you in semantic search.** AI-powered recruiter/partner search ranks by depth and relevance.
4. **It establishes credibility before the first message.** Coherent positioning across headline → banner → About → Featured signals seriousness.
5. **It does the work while you sleep.** The profile sells in your absence.

## Self-review framework

Run these against the current profile, ideally on mobile portrait.

### The 6-second test
1. From photo + headline alone, is it clear what you think about and build?
2. Generic job title or specific positioning?
3. Does the banner reinforce a POV, or is it default blue / stock cityscape?

### The About section
4. Do the first ~300 characters front-load search-relevant terms or is it preamble?
5. First-person?
6. Reads like a person with a perspective, or a CV / pitch deck?
7. Pitches a service (lead-gen) or conveys credibility and POV (locked mode)?
8. Any banned words? (delve, leverage, journey, transformative, etc.)
9. UK English throughout (organise, optimise, behaviour)?
10. Is "lead with the answer" broken with throat-clearing intros?

### Featured
11. Populated or empty?
12. Best content, or sales artefacts?
13. Anything sales-oriented (Calendly, lead magnet) that must come out?

### Skills and Experience
14. Are the top 3 pinned skills the ones a relevant searcher would use?
15. Does the job title read searchable as written, without inflation?

### Banner
16. Carries the positioning ("Bringing ideas to life") or visual identity?
17. Retains the KPMG subtitle for credibility anchoring (locked)?
18. Clean at 1584×396?

### Activity
19. Recent activity coherent with the brand, or off-topic noise?
20. Old company-page reshares crowding out original content?

## High-level improvements (from what's locked)

### Headline rebuild
- Lead with role and KPMG anchor for institutional credibility.
- Signal the content territory: building, AI, emerging tech, products.
- Reflect personality without sales pitch. Avoid "I help X do Y".
- Include searchable keywords the audience would type.

### About section rewrite
- First 200 characters: state what you think about and build. Front-load keywords.
- Reframe from sales-funnel to credibility and POV. First-person throughout.
- Apply voice rules end-to-end (UK English, banned words removed, lead with the answer, no em dashes).
- End with a credibility signal or POV statement, not a CTA to book.

### Featured section
- Populate with three high-performing carousels once they exist.
- For now, best existing content, or leave empty until carousels ship (empty beats off-brand).
- No Calendly. No lead magnets. No "work with me" links.

### Banner
- Rebuild on the new visual identity (separate task). "Bringing ideas to life" tagline. KPMG subtitle retained. Squircle / gradient system visible.

### Activity hygiene
- Audit recent comments and reshares; anything contradicting the positioning gets less attention going forward. Forward-looking discipline, not deletion.

## Rewrite templates

### Headline (220 character limit)

**Template A — Role-anchored, builder-tilted:**
> [Role Title] at KPMG Global Incubator. Building [specific focus, e.g. ventures in AI / emerging tech]. Writing about what works, what doesn't, and why.

**Template B — POV-led, role second:**
> Bringing ideas to life. [Role Title] at KPMG Global Incubator. Building in AI, emerging tech, and product.

**Template C — Outcome-led without sales pitch:**
> [Role Title], KPMG Global Incubator. Builder of [X] and [Y]. Sharper takes on AI, product, and what it actually takes to ship.

Test all three on mobile. Keep the one that reads strongest at thumbnail size. Whitespace in a headline is a feature.

### About section (2,600 characters; first 300 are what matters)

```
[Opening — first 200 chars, front-loaded with keywords and POV]
I think about [topic 1], [topic 2], and [topic 3]. I write what I'm learning as I go.

[Credibility paragraph — what you do and where]
At KPMG's Global Incubator, I work on [specific focus]. Before this I [relevant prior experience].

[POV paragraph — what you actually think]
The thing I'm most interested in right now is [specific take or question]. [One or two sentences of position, not pitch.]

[What you write about and why]
On here I post about [pillar 1], [pillar 2], and [pillar 3]. Mostly the parts I had to learn the hard way.

[Close — credibility signal, not CTA]
[London-based / current focus / what's on the workbench]. Sometimes wrong, occasionally useful.
```

**Notes:**
- The opening sentence is the highest-stakes piece. Lead with the answer. No "Welcome to my profile".
- Avoid the banned list. Run a find-and-replace before posting.
- UK English throughout. No em dashes. No CTAs to book or contact.
- "Sometimes wrong, occasionally useful" is a sample tone; adjust to taste. A line signalling self-awareness lands harder than corporate polish.

### Featured section (three pinned items)

For now (before carousels ship): best existing original post; a second substantive piece; leave the third empty rather than pin something off-brand. Once carousels run: top carousel; second-best carousel; a signature thought piece. Rotate quarterly.

### Banner

Specs locked: 1584×396, RGB, 72 DPI. Gradient or fractal-glass background. "Bringing ideas to life" wordmark in Fraunces. "KPMG Global Incubator" subtitle in Inter, retained for institutional anchor.
